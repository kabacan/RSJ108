Kristiana Abacan's Tips on How to Create a Successful Promotional Video

An Instruction Challenge for JOUR108

How to make an awesome professional video that people will love

Assembly time: 48 hours
Awesome rating: 99.99% awesome

Are you interested in making videos? Don't know where to start. Well, follow these steps and your video will surely capture the attention of the audience!

  • Things You'll Need

  • Paper and pen
  • A good camera
  • People willing to act in the video
  • Any type of editing software
  • Computer
  • Social media

  1. State the company's vision.

    What is your company about? What is your focus; what is your mission? What makes your company go out and help others or create products for different consumers?

  2. Include corporate employees.

    There are real people working to create products or to reach out and talk to consumers and clients. Let your audience know who is working for you. Allow employees to share their take on the company and their individual vision and how it relates to the company. Including employees from different divisions/offices shows how far your company reaches out.

    Establish a personal tone to capture the work environment and business motto.

  3. Branding the CEO.

    Give a face to the name. Let the audience see who is in charge of the corporation, whether it's a fresh clip or one taken from previous interviews.

  4. Give public footage.

    If an employee has been featured in an interview or the CEO has been on TV, show that footage to show that your company gets out there and is involved. Even footage during events or meeting other brands is important.

    Be sure to create purposeful footage of employees and get establishing shots.

  5. Always get to the point.

    Try not to stray from the point. With or without narrative, don't fill the video with fluff. Whether it's short or long, make sure the details of your company are being discussed in an impactful but brief way.

  6. Hit the highlights of your company or brand.

    Flashing logos of different partners, placing the business' starting year in the corner, showing clips from important events or projectsâ€”whatever shows your company's versatility and efficiency should be interesting and added into the video.

  7. Explore some more unique approaches.

    If the typical narrative isn't your fancy or if you're just not down with the flying logos and spinning text, you can also try some interesting alternatives that aren't quite as serious and are more personal. Like videos asking "Why do we work here?", day in the life, then and now videos or customer demonstration.
